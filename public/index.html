Solar Eclipse

A total solar eclipse happens when the Moon passes between the Sun and Earth, completely blocking the face of the Sun. People located in the center of the Moon’s shadow when it hits Earth will experience a total eclipse. The next total solar eclipse in the U.S. will be on  April 8, 2024.

Lunar Eclipse

During a total lunar eclipse, Earth completely blocks direct sunlight from reaching the Moon, casting it into Earth's shadow. The Moon can appear reddish due to Earth's atmosphere bending sunlight around the Earth and onto the Moon. The next occurring on March 14, 2025 it would be partial and penumbral. .
